    Music To Fall Asleep To : does exactly what it say on the tin

    Chillout : the next tempo up. A general bucket of stuff I nab from to throw in other tag radios. Also works as 'easy listening' when I'm busy and want background music. Includes indie, rock, folk, electronic.

    Just A Bit of Fun : the name comes from Rob Brydon's catchphrase in his talk show. Another bucket of stuff, this time uptempo. To be played loud and proud!

    My Gang - Featured Artists : also in my sig. New albums this year, some surprises I rather like. Work in progress.

    Chiller Cabinet : based on my favourite radio show. Ambient, minimalist, movie music. Meant to be played in the middle of the night, a little bit scary sometimes, othertimes very beautiful. Has a distinct theme and sound. Anything else is just 'chillout' (see above).

    The Arctic Circle : Another project from the producer of The Chiller Cabinet - a London-based music collective. Ambient, contemporary classical, electronic, folk, indie pop.

    i literally have a million of these. well, by "literally" i mean "figuratively".

    location location location – named for the TV show, for artist names, track and album titles featuring place names.

    h5n1 – designed to mark the bird flu epidemic that was going to kill us all, but never came. Useful in times of wheezing chickens, cordoned-off turkey farms, and the likes.

    we need food – title taken from a sample in "Beef Rapp" by MF DOOM. For food-related tracks, artists and albums.

    gratuitous and fun swearing – self-explanatory.

    fisting for compliments – all the tracks we played on my old radio show of the same name.

    any club that would have me as a member – named for the Groucho quote (and slightly misquoted, i might add), this is for anything with the word 'Club' in it.

    songs with the same names as other artists – doesn't matter whether the song was named after the band or the band after the song or neither.

    religious figures – music whose name mentions folks from religion.

    historical figures – its secular counterpart.

    exactly what it says on the tin – for songs whose title exactly describes their contents.

    over thirty minutes – meditation/drugs?

    your emotions – named for the DK's song, for music including the name of a human emotion.

    Not mine but good:
    under a minute/under two minutes – "Why can't people understand / i got a short attention span?" (Fizzy Bangers)
